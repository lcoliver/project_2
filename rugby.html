• RUGBY: A Beginner's Guide

  • Rugby has 15 positions on each side. Each have their own duties and require different skills and body types.

    Positions

  • The rugby ball can be moved in one of three ways: running, passing, or kicking. If passed, the ball MUST be passed backwards.

    Moving the Ball

  • Tackling

    In order to stop the offensive team's progress down the field, the defense must make tackles. Rugby tackles are made with the shoulder and there are certain requirements for a proper tackle that players must observe in order to avoid injury and penalty.

  • Play in rugby only stops when the ball goes out of bounds, or a player commits an infraction. To restart play, there are line outs and scrums.

    Resetting Play

  • The goal in rugby is to score the most points in the 80 minute game. In order to do this, a team must work their way to the opposite end of the field to score.
